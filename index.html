South Asians face a unique and elevated risk profile that traditional tools often miss. Standard risk scores like Framingham were developed largely on Western populations and frequently underestimate the danger for South Asians.

The "Thin-Fat" Phenotype

South Asians often have a normal BMI but high visceral adipose tissue (internal fat). This hidden fat releases inflammatory markers that damage arteries.

Lipoprotein(a)

Elevated levels of Lp(a), a genetically determined risk factor that causes clotting and plaque, are significantly more prevalent in this population.

Insulin Resistance

A predisposition to Type 2 Diabetes creates a "metabolic storm" that accelerates atherosclerosis (hardening of arteries) decades earlier.

Inside the Black Box

How Machine Learning sees what doctors might miss.

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs)

CNNs treat ECGs like images. They scan the waveform for micro-patterns‚Äîtiny fluctuations in the ST-segment or T-wave‚Äîthat are invisible to the human eye but signal early ischemia.

Transformer Models

Originally built for language, Transformers analyze the "grammar" of the heart beat. They look at long-term dependencies in the heart rhythm to predict future events, not just current state.

  • Multimodal Fusion: Combining ECG data + Genetic Markers + Clinical History.
